Biofuel as a energy source

Biofuel is any fuel derived from biomass, which includes plant or algae material and animal waste. Unlike fossil fuels such as petroleum, coal, and natural gas, biofuels are considered a renewable energy source because their feedstock material can be replenished readily.

Environmental Benefits: Biofuels are advocated as a cost-effective and environmentally benign alternative to fossil fuels. They can help reduce greenhouse gas emissions and dependence on non-renewable resources.

Challenges: Critics express concerns about the economic and environmental costs associated with biofuel production and the potential impact on arable land used for food production.
